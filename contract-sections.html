Commercial Base

The 1872 Foundation

The Indian Contract Act of 1872 was enacted to regulate mercantile transactions and provide a set of rules for making agreements. Before this, trade was governed by diverse customs. This Act brought a uniform legal standard, essential for the growth of business and trade in the Indian subcontinent.

Essentials

Offer & Acceptance

The Act defines what makes an agreement a "Contract". Key sections include Section 2 (Definitions), Section 10 (What agreements are contracts), and Section 73 (Compensation for breach). It emphasizes on "Free Consent", meaning no contract is valid if made under pressure or fraud.

Modern Use

Business Law

In today's world, from clicking "I Agree" on an app to signing a multi-million dollar deal, everything is governed by this Act. It provides the legal safety net that ensures if one party fails to fulfill their promise, the other can seek justice and compensation in court.
