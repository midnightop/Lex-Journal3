The 1872 Foundation

The Indian Contract Act of 1872 was enacted to regulate mercantile transactions and provide a set of rules for making agreements. This Act brought a uniform legal standard, essential for the growth of business in India.

Essentials

Offer & Acceptance

The Act defines what makes an agreement a "Contract". Key highlights include Section 10 (What agreements are contracts) and the requirement of "Free Consent", ensuring no contract is valid if made under fraud.

Modern Use

Business Law

From digital terms to multi-million dollar deals, everything is governed by this Act. It provides the legal safety net that ensures justice and compensation if one party fails to fulfill their promise.
