Hacked? The Legal Blueprint to Recover Your Social Media Assets in India

Anusmita Das

Durgapur Institute of Legal Studies | Kazi Nazrul University

In 2026, your social media presence isn't just a hobby—it's digital property. Whether it's an Instagram business account, a YouTube channel with thousands of subscribers, or a personal Facebook profile, a hack is more than an inconvenience; it's a cybercrime. In India, the law has evolved to treat these breaches with the same gravity as physical trespassing.

Immediate Response

Before the law steps in, you must freeze the damage. Hackers often change your email and phone number within 60 seconds. Use the "Revert Change" link sent to your original email immediately. This is your most powerful technical window for recovery.

1. Legal Framework: BNS & IT Act

Hacking is a punishable offense under Indian law. Understanding which sections apply to you is crucial for a strong police complaint:

  • Section 66 of IT Act: Punishes "hacking" with up to 3 years of imprisonment or a fine of up to ₹5 lakh.
  • Section 66C: Specifically deals with Identity Theft (stealing your login credentials).
  • BNS Provisions: Under the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), cyber-extortion and digital fraud carry even stricter penalties and expedited investigation timelines.

2. The 3-Step Recovery Process

Follow this hierarchy to ensure you have both your account back and a legal paper trail:

Step A: File a Cyber Complaint

Log on to cybercrime.gov.in. Register a complaint and keep the 15-digit acknowledgement number. This serves as your primary evidence when communicating with Instagram or YouTube's legal teams.

Step B: The "Identity Verification" Appeal

For Instagram/Facebook, use the Video Selfie Verification tool. For YouTube, contact "TeamYouTube" on X (formerly Twitter) with your Channel ID and the date of the hack.

Step C: The Legal Notice

If the platform (Meta/Google) remains unresponsive for more than 72 hours, have a lawyer send a Formal Legal Notice to their Indian Grievance Officer. Under the Intermediary Guidelines, they are legally bound to act on "Identity Spoofing" within 24-36 hours.

3. Platform-Specific Evidence

Gather these details before visiting the Cyber Cell:

Platform Evidence Needed Legal Contact
Instagram Screenshot of "Email Changed" alert Grievance Officer (India)
YouTube Original AdSense ID / Video URLs Google India Support
X (Twitter) IP Logs / Login History X Corp India Officer

4. What to do if the Hacker Extorts You?

Never pay. If a hacker demands money (Ransomware) to return your account, this constitutes a higher grade of offense under BNS Section 308 (Extortion). Document the WhatsApp/DM conversation and provide it to the police. Paying only signals that you are a "high-value target" for future hacks.
