Section 103 of BNS: How the Murder Law differs from IPC 302

Rey

Surendranath Law College | University of Calcutta

For over a century, the Indian legal system was identified by a single digit when it came to the ultimate crime—IPC Section 302. However, as of 2026, with the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) fully operational, this landmark section has been replaced by Section 103 of the BNS.

1. Section 103(1): The Core Punishment

In simple terms, if an individual commits murder, they are charged under Section 103(1). While the legal definition of murder remains largely consistent with the old code, the statutory reference has changed. This section covers the intentional killing of a human being.

Punishment: Death Penalty or Life Imprisonment, along with a mandatory fine.

2. Mob Lynching: The Landmark Addition in Section 103(2)

This is the most critical update in the new code. Previously, the IPC did not have a specific provision for 'Mob Lynching' (where a group of five or more people commit murder). Under the BNS, **Section 103(2)** has been specifically designed to address this social evil.

  • If a group of 5 or more persons acting in concert commits murder on the grounds of Race, Caste, Community, Sex, or Language...
  • Each member of that group shall be punished with the **Death Penalty** or **Life Imprisonment**.
Legal Feature IPC (Old) BNS (New 2026)
Primary Section Section 302 Section 103
Mob Lynching No dedicated sub-section Specific Clause 103(2)
Minimum Penalty Life Imprisonment Life Imprisonment

3. Has the Definition of Murder Changed?

Technically, no. Section 101 of the BNS defines murder just as IPC Section 300 did. The four essential ingredients—Intent, Knowledge, and Bodily Injury sufficient to cause death—remain the standard for prosecution in 2026.

Key Takeaway for Law Students

Law students and practitioners must now transition away from citing IPC 302. However, landmark judicial precedents like the 'Rarest of Rare' doctrine from Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab remain valid and applicable when interpreting the gravity of the offense under the new Section 103.

4. Conclusion: A Modern Legislative Shift

Section 103 is not merely a change in numbering; it is a legislative effort to recognize and penalize crimes like mob violence explicitly. As we integrate digital evidence under the BSA in 2026, these updated sections ensure a more robust and faster path to justice.
