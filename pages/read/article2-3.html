The 2/3rd Clause: Is Raghav Chadha's Switch to BJP Constitutionally Valid?

Breaking down the legal shield that protected 7 AAP MPs from disqualification.

Rey

Constitutional Expert | CU

THE NEWS:

Raghav Chadha, Harbhajan Singh, and 5 other Rajya Sabha MPs have officially joined the BJP. This "wholesale merger" has changed the Rajya Sabha equations overnight.

1. The Law in Simple English

In India, the Anti-Defection Law (10th Schedule) was made to stop leaders from changing parties like clothes. Usually, if an MP leaves their party, they lose their seat in Parliament.

However, there is a "Magic Number" in the law. If two-thirds (2/3rd) of the party's members leave together to join another party, the law calls it a Merger, not Defection. In this case, no one loses their seat.

Why 7 is the Winning Number for BJP

Let's look at the math that kept Raghav Chadha's seat safe:

Total AAP Rajya Sabha MPs: 10
2/3rd Requirement (66.6%): 6.66 (7 MPs)
MPs who joined BJP: 7

Since exactly 7 MPs moved, they hit the 70% mark, comfortably crossing the 2/3rd legal hurdle.

2. Is it Unconstitutional?

The short answer: No, it is legally valid, but ethically debated.

Legally, the 91st Amendment (2003) specifically allows this. The Supreme Court has said that if the required number is met, the Speaker/Chairman cannot disqualify them. However, people often call it "unconstitutional" because it goes against the "voter's trust."

3. Keywords You Should Know (Google Trends)

If you are following this news, here are the legal terms trending right now:

  • The Whip: The official order of a party. If an MP disobeys the "Whip," they are disqualified. By merging, Chadha's team created their own new "Whip" under BJP.
  • Kihoto Hollohan Case: A famous court case where the SC said the Speaker's decision can be challenged in court if it's biased.

The 2026 Shift: A New Precedent?

This event is unique because it involves a high percentage of a party's upper house strength moving at once. Experts are calling this "Wholesale Politics." While it looks like a clean legal move, the AAP leadership is expected to move to the Supreme Court to challenge the "intent" of the merger.

Summary: Easy Points

  • Raghav Chadha is not disqualified because 70% of his team moved with him.
  • The 10th Schedule protects groups, not individuals.
  • BJP now becomes significantly stronger in the Rajya Sabha.
