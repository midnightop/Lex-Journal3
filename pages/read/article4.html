Case law, also known as common law, is a legal system where court decisions in prior cases (precedents) are used to guide future rulings in similar situations.

The core mechanism

Courts analyze past decisions to determine how similar legal principles should be applied to new cases. It ensures that the law isn't just words on a page, but a living practice shaped by history.

Main Features of Case Law

Stare Decisis: Following reasoning of past decisions to ensure consistency.

Binding Precedent: Lower courts must follow higher courts' decisions.

Persuasive Precedent: Non-binding influence from other jurisdictions.

Distinguishing: Highlighting factual differences to justify different outcomes.

Important Sub-areas

Contract Law

Landmark cases on consideration and good faith performance.

Tort Law

Defines negligence, product liability, and intentional torts.

Criminal Law

Precedent defines elements of crimes and mens rea (guilty mind).

Constitutional Law

Supreme Court interpretations on federalism and free speech.

Key Concepts & Definitions

Ratio Decidendi

The actual legal reasoning behind a decision—the part that is binding.

Obiter Dictum

Observations made by a judge that are NOT essential or binding.
