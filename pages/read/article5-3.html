New Labour Laws in India: Practical Impact on Salary, PF, and Working Hours

Anusmita Das

Durgapur institute of legal study | kazi Nazrul University

The transition to India's **4 New Labour Codes** marks a pivotal shift in employment law. By merging 29 legacy acts, the 2026 framework aims to modernize the workplace. However, for the average employee, this raises several immediate questions about their take-home pay and daily office life.

1. Salary DNA: The 50% Rule Explained

The "Code on Wages" fundamentally changes how your monthly salary is calculated. This is no longer just a corporate decision; it is a legal mandate.

Basic Pay Cap

Your Basic Salary must now be at least 50% of your total CTC. This eliminates "ghost allowances" used to lower PF contributions.

The PF (Provident Fund) Surge

Since PF is a percentage of Basic Pay, a higher Basic Pay means significantly higher savings. You are essentially forced to build a larger retirement fund.

Reduced In-Hand Salary

As PF contributions increase, your monthly net pay (take-home) might see a slight dip. It is a shift from present liquidity to future security.

2. The New Work Culture: Hours & Overtime

The 2026 framework introduces flexibility that could either lead to longer weekends or longer workdays.

The 12-Hour Shift Option

The weekly limit remains 48 hours, but the daily distribution can change:

  • 4-Day Work Week: Companies can implement 12-hour shifts for 4 days, followed by 3 days off.
  • Overtime Pay: Any hour worked beyond the 48-hour weekly limit must be paid at 2x the normal wage.
  • Consent: These changes generally require mutual consent and cannot be forced overnight.

4. Frequently Asked Questions (SEO-Optimized)

Is the 4-day work week mandatory in India?

No. The 4-day work week is an **option** provided by the new codes. Companies and employees must agree to it. If adopted, the working day will extend to 12 hours to maintain the 48-hour weekly legal limit.

How will the new labour law affect my PF (Provident Fund)?

Because the new codes mandate that Basic Pay must be 50% of your CTC, the base on which PF is calculated increases. This means both employee and employer contributions will rise, resulting in a **larger retirement corpus**.

What is "Retrenchment" under the 2026 Industrial Relations Code?

Retrenchment refers to the termination of services for reasons other than punishment. Under the new code, companies with up to **300 employees** can retrench staff without prior government approval, though they must pay severance and contribute to a Reskilling Fund.

Can women work night shifts under the new codes?

Yes. The Occupational Safety, Health & Working Conditions Code allows women to work in all establishments, including night shifts, provided that the employer ensures **safety, transport, and consent**.

How do I calculate my new in-hand salary for 2026?

To estimate your salary, ensure your Basic Pay is 50% of your CTC. Deduct 12% of that Basic Pay for your PF contribution. The remaining amount (after other tax/allowance deductions) will be your monthly take-home pay.
