New Labour Laws in India: Understanding the 4 Codes & Salary Changes

Anusmita Das

Durgapur institute of legal study | kazi Nazrul University

India is witnessing a historic shift in its industrial landscape. The transition from 29 fragmented central acts to **4 comprehensive Labour Codes** is designed to simplify compliance and boost social security. Whether you are an HR professional or an employee, these changes in 2026 will redefine your workplace rights.

1. The 4 Pillars of New Labour Law

Code on Wages, 2019

Standardizes "Wages" across India. Ensures timely payment and universal minimum wages for both organized and unorganized sectors.

Industrial Relations Code, 2020

Modernizes rules for Strikes, Lockouts, and Retrenchment. Aimed at reducing industrial disputes and fostering ease of business.

Social Security & OSH Codes

Extends EPF and ESI benefits to Gig workers and establishes strict safety standards for hazardous work environments.

2. New Salary Structure 2026: Impact on Take-Home Pay

Under the new definition of wages, the 'Basic Pay' must be at least 50% of the total CTC. This change has two major impacts:

  • Higher PF Contributions: Since PF is calculated on basic pay, your retirement corpus will grow significantly faster.
  • Reduced In-Hand Salary: A higher PF deduction might lead to a slight decrease in the monthly cash-in-hand for some employees.
  • Gratuity Benefits: Gratuity will now be calculated on a higher base, providing better long-term security.

4. Frequently Asked Questions

What is 'Retrenchment' under the new law?

Retrenchment refers to the termination of a worker for any reason other than disciplinary action. Under the new IR Code, companies with up to 300 workers can now proceed with retrenchment without prior government approval.

Are Gig Workers (Zomato/Swiggy) covered?

Yes! For the first time in Indian history, the Social Security Code 2020 brings gig and platform workers under the ambit of social security schemes, including life and disability cover.

Will my working hours increase?

While the daily limit can be extended to 12 hours, the weekly limit remains strictly at 48 hours. This allows for a flexible 4 or 5-day work week depending on the company's policy.
