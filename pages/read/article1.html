Digital Sovereignty: The 2023 Personal Data Protection Act

Arjun Sharma

NLU Delhi | Faculty of Law

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), 2023, marks a watershed moment in India's legal history. After years of deliberation and multiple drafts, the Indian Parliament has finally established a framework that seeks to balance the rights of individuals to protect their personal data with the need to process such data for lawful purposes.

The Concept of "Data Principal"

Unlike previous laws, the DPDPA introduces the term "Data Principal" to refer to the individual whose data is being collected. This shift in terminology is not merely cosmetic; it signifies a fundamental recognition of the individual's ownership over their digital footprint.

"Personal data shall be processed only for a lawful purpose for which the Data Principal has given her consent." — Section 6, DPDPA 2023.

Key Challenges in Implementation

While the Act is robust, the establishment of the Data Protection Board of India remains the most critical step. The independence of this board will determine whether the Act becomes a shield for citizens or a tool for corporate compliance.

Furthermore, the "Right to Erasure" and the "Right to Correction" are now legally enforceable. This means companies can no longer hold onto your data indefinitely once the purpose of collection has been served.
