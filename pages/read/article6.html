How to File an Online FIR in India: The Ultimate Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

Payel Dhibar

Kingston Law College | WBSU

Losing a mobile phone or being a victim of a cybercrime is stressful. Most citizens avoid the police station due to the perceived complexity. However, the Online FIR system in India has been revolutionized in 2026 to make justice accessible to everyone. In this guide, we break down how to use the CCTNS portal and state police websites to report crimes instantly.

Quick Check: Is your crime eligible for e-FIR?

Online FIRs are highly effective for Non-Cognizable Reports (NCR). For serious crimes, you can file an "e-Complaint" which acts as a precursor to a formal investigation.

  • Lost Mobile or SIM Cards
  • Missing Documents (Voter ID, Passport)
  • Vehicle Theft (Selected States)
  • Cyber Fraud & Online Harassment

The 5-Step Digital Filing Process

Step 01

Identify Your Jurisdiction Portal

Police in India is a 'State Subject'. This means a resident of Kolkata must use the West Bengal/Kolkata Police portal, while a resident of Delhi uses the Delhi Police app. Use the table below for direct access.

Step 02

Aadhaar-Linked Authentication

In 2026, most portals use Aadhaar-based OTP for instant verification. This eliminates the need to visit the station just to verify your identity. Ensure your mobile number is linked to your ID.

Step 03

Drafting the Incident Report

When writing your complaint, follow the 4W Rule: When did it happen? Where did it happen? Who is involved? What exactly was taken or done?

Pro SEO Tip: Tracking Your Complaint

Always save the Acknowledgement Number. In the new BNSS (Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita) era, police are mandated to provide digital updates on the status of your FIR within 90 days.

Common FAQs

Can I file an online FIR for a stolen car?

Yes, in major metros like Delhi and Kolkata, e-FIRs for vehicle theft are integrated with insurance providers for faster claims.

Is an e-FIR valid in court?

Absolutely. A digitally signed e-FIR carries the same legal weight as a physical one under the IT Act and BNSS.
