Social Reform

The 1955 Reform

The Hindu Marriage Act was enacted in 1955 as part of the Hindu Code Bills. Before this, Hindu marriage was considered an indissoluble sacrament. The Act was brought to codify laws, introduce monogamy, and provide a legal path for divorce, bringing social equality and protecting the rights of women within the community.

Key Conditions

Validity & Rituals

Key points include Section 5 (Conditions for marriage), Section 7 (Ceremonies like Saptapadi), and Section 13 (Grounds for Divorce). It also covers Restitution of Conjugal Rights (Section 9), which is a unique and often debated legal remedy in Indian family law.

Purpose

Personal Liberty

This Act ensures that personal laws are not just based on ancient texts but are aligned with modern constitutional values. It provides a structured legal framework for maintenance, alimony, and child custody, ensuring that family disputes are resolved with dignity in a court of law.
