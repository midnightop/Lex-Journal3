Social Reform

The 1955 Reform

The Hindu Marriage Act of 1955 codified laws to introduce monogamy and provide a legal path for divorce. It brought social equality and protected the rights of women within the community.

Key Conditions

Validity & Rituals

Key points include Section 5 (Conditions for marriage), Section 7 (Ceremonies like Saptapadi), and Section 13 (Grounds for Divorce). It also covers Restitution of Conjugal Rights (Section 9).

Purpose

Personal Liberty

This Act aligns personal laws with modern constitutional values. It provides a structured framework for maintenance, alimony, and custody, ensuring disputes are resolved with dignity.
