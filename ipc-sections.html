Colonial Legacy

The 1860 Origin

The IPC was drafted in 1860 by Thomas Babington Macaulay. It was created to provide a uniform criminal law across India, replacing diverse local systems during the British era.

Structure

Key Provisions

The IPC contains 511 sections covering crimes from those against the State to offenses affecting the human body and property. Its definition of "Mens Rea" (Guilty Mind) remains a legal masterpiece.

Evolution

Current Standing

While transitioning into the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), the IPC remains the governing statute for crimes committed prior to the new law's implementation. It is the most cited document in Indian criminal history.
