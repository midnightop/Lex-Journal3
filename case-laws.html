Constitutional • 1973

Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala

Basic Structure Doctrine

"Parliament cannot alter the essential features of the Constitution."

Context: This 13-judge bench case (largest ever) decided the extent of Parliament's power to amend the Constitution under Article 368.

Verdict: The court held that while Parliament has wide powers, it cannot touch the 'Basic Structure'—like Secularism, Democracy, and Federalism.

Article 21 • 1978

Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India

Personal Liberty

"Any law taking away liberty must be just, fair, and reasonable."

Context: Maneka Gandhi's passport was impounded without a hearing. She challenged it as a violation of freedom of movement.

Verdict: SC established that 'Procedure established by law' must also be 'Due process of law', expanding the scope of Article 21 immensely.
