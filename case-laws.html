Constitutional 1973

Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala

AIR 1973 SC 1461 • Landmark Bench

"The Parliament has the power to amend the Constitution, but it cannot touch its 'Basic Structure'."

Context: Challenged the Parliament's power to take away Fundamental Rights using amendments.

Verdict: SC ruled that features like Democracy, Secularism, and Judicial Review are part of the 'Basic Structure' and can never be deleted.

Article 21 1978

Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India

AIR 1978 SC 597 • Personal Liberty

"Life is not just physical existence; it includes the right to live with human dignity."

Context: Government impounded Maneka Gandhi's passport without giving a valid reason.

Verdict: Court expanded Article 21, saying any law taking away liberty must be 'Just, Fair, and Reasonable'.

Women Rights 1997

Vishaka v. State of Rajasthan

AIR 1997 SC 3011 • Workplace Safety

"Workplace sexual harassment is a violation of Fundamental Rights."

Context: A social worker was gang-raped, and there were no laws to protect women at workplaces.

Verdict: SC gave 'Vishaka Guidelines' which later became the POSH Act, 2013.
