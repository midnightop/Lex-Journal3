Background & History

How it was Formed?

The Constitution of India was adopted on 26th November 1949 and came into effect on 26th January 1950. It took 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days to complete. The main reason for its creation was to transform India from a British Colony into a Sovereign Democratic Republic, ensuring justice and equality for all citizens after centuries of colonial rule.

Key Features

Main Pillars

The key highlights include the Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35), Directive Principles, and the separation of powers between the Judiciary, Executive, and Legislature. It is the longest written constitution in the world, originally containing 395 articles and 8 schedules, which has now grown to over 448 articles.

Why it Matters?

The Modern Use

Today, the Constitution acts as the supreme shield for every Indian. It protects us from arbitrary state power and gives us the right to move the Supreme Court (Article 32) if our rights are violated. It is a living document that evolves with time through amendments to meet the needs of a modern India.
