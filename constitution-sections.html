Background & History

How it was Formed?

The Constitution of India was adopted on 26th November 1949 and came into effect on 26th January 1950. It took 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days to complete. It transformed India into a Sovereign Democratic Republic.

Key Features

Main Pillars

The key highlights include the Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35), Directive Principles, and the separation of powers. It is the longest written constitution in the world, with over 448 articles today.

Why it Matters?

The Modern Use

The Constitution acts as a supreme shield. It protects us from arbitrary state power and grants us the right to move the Supreme Court (Article 32) if our rights are violated. It is a living document that evolves with time.
