National Law

Constitution

India, 1950 • 448 Articles

"The fundamental framework that governs the Republic of India."

  • • Grundnorm: Supreme law of India.
  • • Federalism: Dual polity with unitary bias.
  • • Fundamental Rights: Guaranteed by Part III.
  • • DPSP: Directive principles for social justice.
  • • Judicial Supremacy: Power of Judicial Review.
  • • Writ Jurisdiction: Art 32 & 226 protections.
  • • Secularism: Equality for all religions.
  • • Amendment: Flexibility via Art 368.
  • • Democratic Republic: Power to the people.
  • • Emergency Powers: Safeguards for the state.

New Law

BNS

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023

"Replacing colonial-era IPC with a victim-centric approach."

  • • Decolonization: Removes British terminology.
  • • Community Service: New punishment type.
  • • Terrorism: Clearly defined for the first time.
  • • Organized Crime: Strict penalties introduced.
  • • Gender Neutrality: Applied to many offenses.
  • • Victim Rights: Faster judicial processes.
  • • Digital Evidence: Broadened legal scope.
  • • Zero FIR: Mandatory filing protocols.
  • • Mob Lynching: Specific criminal provisions.
  • • Sedition: Replaced with Treason focus.

Criminal Code

IPC

Penal Code, 1860 • 511 Sections

"The principal criminal code of India defining offenses."

  • • Codification: Lord Macaulay's legacy.
  • • Mens Rea: Essential guilty mind concept.
  • • Punishments: Range from fine to death.
  • • Murder: Detailed under Section 300.
  • • Theft: Elements of dishonest intent.
  • • Culpable Homicide: Distinct from murder.
  • • Exceptions: General defenses (76-106).
  • • Abetment: Holding accessories liable.
  • • Conspiracy: Criminal agreement focus.
  • • Public Order: Crimes against the state.

Family Law

HMA

Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

"The primary law governing marriage and divorce among Hindus."

  • • Codification: Uniformity in personal law.
  • • Monogamy: Legal requirement of one spouse.
  • • Divorce: Grounds like cruelty & desertion.
  • • Restitution: Conjugal rights (Section 9).
  • • Nullity: Void and voidable marriages.
  • • Maintenance: Rights of spouse (Sec 24/25).
  • • Custody: Welfare of minor children.
  • • Consent: Age and mental health rules.
  • • Ceremonies: Saptapadi and legal rites.
  • • Jurisdiction: Family court procedures.

Civil Law

Contract Act

Indian Contract Act, 1872

"Governing the formation and enforceability of agreements."

  • • Proposal: Essential starting point.
  • • Acceptance: Absolute and unqualified rules.
  • • Consideration: Quid pro quo requirement.
  • • Capacity: Rules for minors/unsound mind.
  • • Free Consent: Absence of fraud or coercion.
  • • Breach: Consequences of non-performance.
  • • Damages: Compensation for losses.
  • • Indemnity: Protection against liability.
  • • Guarantee: Three-party safety netting.
  • • Agency: Rules for acting on behalf.
