Article 32 & 226 Powerhouses

Habeas Corpus

"To have the body"

01

The most powerful weapon for personal liberty. It challenges illegal detention, whether by the State or a private individual.

Landmark Precedent

ADM Jabalpur v. Shivkant Shukla (1976)

Defined the scope of liberty even during national emergencies.

Mandamus

"We Command"

02

A command to perform a public duty. Issued against public officials, governments, or corporations failing to act.

Landmark Precedent

Gujarat State Financial Corp. v. Lotus Hotels

Court enforced the principle that public bodies must act fairly.

Quo Warranto

"By What Authority"

03

Checks the legality of a person's claim to a public office. It prevents people from holding offices they aren't qualified for.

Landmark Precedent

University of Mysore v. Govinda Rao

Clarified that the office must be of 'public nature' for this writ.

Certiorari

"To Be Certified"

04

Quashes an order already passed by a lower court or authority. It is "curative" – it fixes a wrong decision.

Landmark Precedent

A.K. Kraipak v. Union of India (1969)

Landmark for applying writ jurisdiction to administrative decisions.

Prohibition

"Stay Order"

05

Issued while the case is pending. It stops a lower court from continuing a trial that is outside its jurisdiction.

Landmark Precedent

East India Commercial Co. v. Collector

Established the power to stop proceedings at the threshold.

Article 32: Only for Fundamental Rights.
Article 226: Wider powers for any legal right.
