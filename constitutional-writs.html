Habeas Corpus

"To have the body"

Fundamental remedy against unlawful detention by State or private entities.

Key Technical Points

Can be filed by a friend or relative on behalf of the detained person.
Cannot be issued if the detention is under a lawful order of the court.
Primary goal is immediate release from illegal custody.
Landmark Case

ADM Jabalpur v. Shivkant Shukla (1976)

Mandamus

"We Command"

Directs a public official or body to perform a duty they have legally failed to do.

Key Technical Points

The applicant must have a specific legal right to the performance of the duty.
Cannot be issued against the President or State Governors.
Cannot be issued for purely private or contractual obligations.
Landmark Case

Gujarat State Financial Corp. v. Lotus Hotels

Quo Warranto

"By What Authority"

Asks a person to prove under what legal authority they hold a public office.

Key Technical Points

Used only for offices of a public nature created by statute/Constitution.
Prevents 'usurpation' (illegal taking) of a public office.
Can be sought by any interested person, not necessarily the aggrieved.
Landmark Case

University of Mysore v. Govinda Rao

Certiorari

"To Be Certified"

Issued to quash an order passed by a lower court or tribunal due to lack of jurisdiction.

Key Technical Points

It is both 'Preventive' and 'Curative' in nature.
Issued when there is an "Error of Law apparent on the face of the record."
Traditionally against judicial bodies, now also against administrative ones.
Landmark Case

A.K. Kraipak v. Union of India (1969)

Prohibition

"Stay Order"

Issued to stop a lower court from continuing a trial that is outside its legal power.

Key Technical Points

Unlike Certiorari, it is issued while the case is still pending.
Primary aim is to prevent a court from usurping jurisdiction.
It "forbids" the lower body to proceed further.
Landmark Case

East India Commercial Co. v. Collector

Core Difference

Art 32

Limited to Fundamental Rights enforcement.

Art 226

Wider scope: FRs + any legal right.
