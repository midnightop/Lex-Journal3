Habeas Corpus

"To have the body"

Fundamental remedy against unlawful detention by State or private entities.

Key Technical Points

Filed by anyone on behalf of the detained person.
Usage: To challenge illegal arrest or preventive detention.
Not applicable for detention under a court's lawful order.
Judgment 01

ADM Jabalpur v. Shivkant Shukla (1976)

Limited the writ during Emergency (later overruled).

Judgment 02

Sunil Batra v. Delhi Admin (1980)

Used to protect prisoners from custodial torture.

Mandamus

"We Command"

Directs a public official or body to perform a duty they have legally failed to do.

Key Technical Points

Usage: To enforce statutory duties or legal rights.
Cannot be issued against the President or Governors.
The duty must be mandatory, not discretionary.
Judgment 01

Gujarat State Fin. Corp v. Lotus Hotels

Enforced the state's duty to fulfill contractual promises.

Judgment 02

Mani Subrat Jain v. State of Haryana

Clarified that a clear legal right is mandatory for this writ.

Quo Warranto

"By What Authority"

Asks a person to prove under what legal authority they hold a public office.

Key Technical Points

Usage: To challenge illegal appointments in public offices.
Office must be of a substantive and public nature.
Filed to prevent usurpation of public office.
Judgment 01

University of Mysore v. Govinda Rao

Set the standard for challenging illegal academic appointments.

Judgment 02

G.D. Karkare v. T.L. Shevde

Held that any citizen can file this writ (Public Interest).

Certiorari

"To Be Certified"

Issued to quash an order passed by a lower court or tribunal due to lack of jurisdiction.

Key Technical Points

Usage: To correct "Errors of Law" apparent on the face of record.
Curative in nature; quashes the final illegal order.
Applicable to quasi-judicial and administrative bodies.
Judgment 01

A.K. Kraipak v. Union of India

Applied to administrative orders violating Natural Justice.

Judgment 02

Province of Bombay v. Khushaldas Advani

Clarified difference between judicial and ministerial acts.

Prohibition

"Stay Order"

Issued to stop a lower court from continuing a trial that is outside its legal power.

Key Technical Points

Usage: To stop a pending case before a final order is passed.
Preventive in nature; stops excess of jurisdiction.
Issued only against judicial or quasi-judicial bodies.
Judgment 01

East India Commercial Co. v. Collector

Stopping authorities from acting beyond statute powers.

Judgment 02

S. Govinda Menon v. Union of India

Confirmed writ usage in ongoing departmental inquiries.

Core Difference

Art 32

Limited to Fundamental Rights enforcement.

Art 226

Wider scope: FRs + any legal right.
