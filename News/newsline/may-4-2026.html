Weekly Legal Brief: The Transition to BNS and E-Challan Jurisprudence

A detailed look at the procedural shifts in Indian Criminal Law and the evolving standards for digital evidence in traffic violations.

01 The BNSS Procedural Overhaul

The Supreme Court recently addressed a series of petitions clarifying how pending investigations must move from the old CrPC to the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS). The court ruled that if an offense was committed under the IPC but the investigation began after the new laws came into force, the procedural steps (like search, seizure, and arrest) must strictly follow the BNSS.

Crucially, the right to statutory bail under the new laws has been held as a fundamental protection that cannot be bypassed during this administrative transition. For students, it is vital to understand that while the "Crime" is IPC, the "Process" is now BNSS.

02 E-Challan Evidence Standards (MV Act)

With the rise of AI-powered traffic cameras, several High Courts have observed that "automatic challans" cannot be the sole basis for conviction in contested cases. Specifically, under Section 184 (Dangerous Driving), the court has mandated that authorities must provide clear video evidence showing the nature of the danger, rather than just a still photo of a vehicle.

This week, new guidelines also streamline the process for Section 194 (Overloading), where digital records from toll-plaza weight sensors will now be automatically linked to a vehicle's digital permit, allowing for real-time suspensions.

03 Mens Rea in Cyber Financial Crimes

A landmark interpretation of Actus Non Facit Reum Nisi Mens Sit Rea was applied to "Mule Account" holders. The court emphasized that innocent victims whose bank accounts were used by scammers without their knowledge should not face the same harsh penalties as the actual conspirators.

Student Note

"While studying these cases, always look for the 'Intent'. Modern cyber law is increasingly focusing on the distinction between negligence and actual criminal intent."
