Origin

The Transition

The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) was introduced in 2023 to replace the British-era Indian Penal Code of 1860. The primary reason was to decolonize the Indian legal system and move away from a "punishment-centric" approach to a "justice-centric" one.

Core Changes

New Sections

BNS has reduced the number of sections and introduced Community Service as a form of punishment. Key changes include Section 103 for Murder (formerly 302 IPC) and new provisions for organized crime, terrorism, and crimes against women.

Vision

The Impact

The implementation of BNS marks a historic shift in Indian criminal law. It aims to speed up the judicial process and integrate digital evidence more effectively. For law students, it is essential to understand this new framework.
